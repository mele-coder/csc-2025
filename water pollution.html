What is Water Pollution?

Water pollution is the contamination of the body of waters caused by harmful substances making the water toxic for marine biodiversity and human usages. This affects world wide, but some regions like Asia have it more challenging with rivers like Yangtze river in China, Ganges river in India and the Citarum river in Indonesia. Water pollution happens when harmful substances, often chemicals or microorganisms, contaminate streams, lakes, ocean and aquifer. Affecting the water quality negatively and making it toxic to humans and the environment. Water pollution is caused by a variation of things for example animal waste which contains bacteria and pathogens from animals that gets into the water, as well as algae a green substance that are made from plant-like organisms that live in the aquatic ecosystem this causes disruption to marine life as it releases toxins into the water also decreases oxygen levels, including industrial waste when industrial waste water gets dumped into the water dissolving into the water impacting the water source and ecosystems negatively and including oil spills which gets released into these body of waters causing them to be unable to consume. Water pollution causes harm towards the aquatic ecosystems killing marine life and impacting the food chain. Contamination in drinking water can lead to diseases and health problems in humans affecting the human population.
